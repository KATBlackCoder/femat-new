# Taekwondo – Prime Minister Elevated to 4th Dan Black Belt

**Bamako, January 8, 2026** – A historic ceremony took place on Thursday, January 8, 2026 at the Prime Minister's Office. The Prime Minister, Head of Government, Major General Abdoulaye MAÏGA, received a delegation from the Malian Taekwondo Federation (FEMAT) for an official meeting marked by a symbolic and honorary event.

## An Exceptional Honorary Distinction

On this exceptional occasion, the Prime Minister was elevated, on an honorary basis, to the grade of **4th Dan Taekwondo Black Belt**. A Dobok (traditional kimono) and a black belt were officially presented to him, symbols of recognition and adherence to the fundamental values of this martial art.

This distinction reflects the commitment of the country's highest authorities to the development of Taekwondo and sport in general, recognizing the importance of this discipline as a vector for youth education and training.

## A Prestigious Delegation

The delegation was led by the **Secretary General of the Ministry of Youth and Sports**, in charge of Civic Education and Citizen Building. At the head of this delegation was **Dr. Engineer Alioune Badara TRAORÉ**, President of the Malian Taekwondo Federation, 8th Dan Black Belt, an emblematic figure of Malian Taekwondo.

## Speech of Commitment and Recognition

After the customary greetings, the Federation President expressed the **profound gratitude of FEMAT** for the Government's constant efforts in favor of Taekwondo and sport in general.

> "Taekwondo is much more than a sport: it is a school of life, a lever for moral rebuilding and an instrument for youth education, in perfect harmony with the vast project of State Rebuilding undertaken under the leadership of His Excellency Army General Assimi GOÏTA, President of the Transition, Head of State."

The delegation head also thanked the Prime Minister for his **permanent availability** and **constant support** for the Federation's initiatives.

## Prime Minister's Reaction

Speaking, the Prime Minister said he was **deeply honored and fully satisfied** with this distinction. He expressed his pride and gratitude for the presentation of the Dobok and his elevation to 4th Dan, which he received with emotion.

On this occasion, he extended his **New Year 2026 wishes** to the Malian Taekwondo Federation, wishing for **greater recognition of Malian Taekwondo** on the international stage. He also reassured that the Prime Minister's Office remains **fully available** to support the Federation in its missions serving youth.

## Taekwondo Values at the Heart of Education

The Prime Minister strongly recalled that **"a healthy mind in a healthy body"** constitutes an essential foundation for individual and social balance. He praised the role of Taekwondo in the moral, civic and physical training of young people, notably as a tool for prevention against drugs and other harmful substances.

This vision aligns perfectly with the objectives of development and fulfillment of Malian youth carried by the government.

## A Reinforced Pan-Africanist Commitment

Addressing the Federation President, a convinced pan-Africanist, the Head of Government urged him to **pursue his commitment** in the dynamic of the fight for dignity and honor, also carried by the highest authorities of the State, for the benefit of the future of Malian youth.

## An Emblematic Presence

The presence at this meeting of **Mr. Daba Modibo KEÏTA**, double world Taekwondo champion, emblematic figure and source of inspiration for Malian sports youth, should be noted. His presence added a special dimension to this event, symbolizing the excellence and recognition of Malian Taekwondo.

*This historic ceremony marks a new step in strengthening relations between State institutions and the Malian Taekwondo Federation, reinforcing the common commitment to the development of sport and youth education.*
